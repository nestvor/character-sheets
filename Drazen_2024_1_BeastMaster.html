A farmer who became a hermit, who is becoming something else.

Skill Proficiencies: Animal Handling, Survival

Tool Proficiency: Herbalism Kit

Language: Sylvan

Ability Score Increases (+1/+1/+1): Dexterity, Wisdom, Constitution

Origin Feat: Magic Initiate (Druid) — see card below

Equipment: Herbalism kit, druidic focus, traveler's clothes, hand-carved wooden talisman of a stag, leather pouch, clay pipe, 11 gp

Magic Initiate (Druid) Origin Feat

You learn two cantrips and one 1st-level spell from the druid spell list. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for them. You can cast the 1st-level spell once without expending a spell slot; you regain that free casting on a Long Rest. You can also cast it using any spell slots you have.

  • Cantrip 1 — Thorn Whip: melee spell attack at 30 ft, 1d6 piercing, pulls Large-or-smaller target 10 ft toward you.
  • Cantrip 2 — Guidance: touch a willing creature, +1d4 to one ability check made within 1 minute. Concentration.
  • 1st-level — Faerie Fire: 20-ft cube, DEX save DC 13 or outlined; outlined creatures grant advantage on attacks against them for 1 minute. Concentration.

Spell save DC 13 · Spell attack +5 · Wisdom-based · Druidic Focus required

Small & Quick Forest Gnome

Size: Small (3'0"–3'6"). Speed: 30 ft.

Darkvision 60 ft. In dim light you see as if in bright light, in darkness as if in dim light (greyscale only).

Lifespan: Up to ~70 years. Dražen is in his late 40s — middle-aged for his kind.

Gnomish Cunning Forest Gnome

You have advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws against being charmed or frightened, and against magic that would put you to sleep.

Older Gnomadi are known for their stubborn refusal to be moved by glamour. The earth holds him better than fey words.

Speak with Small Beasts Forest Gnome

Through sounds and gestures, you can communicate simple ideas with Small or smaller beasts. Forest gnomes love animals and often have weasels, rabbits, badgers, hedgehogs, and the like as friends.

A foundation for the bond that arrives in full at L3. The land already speaks to him; now he speaks back.

Minor Illusion Forest Gnome

You know the Minor Illusion cantrip. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it.

Create either an image of an object (no larger than a 5-foot cube) or a sound. Lasts 1 minute. Investigation check (vs. INT 10 = DC 10) reveals the deception.

A bird's call to misdirect a sentry. The sound of footsteps in the wrong corridor. A patch of darkness that wasn't there. Small magic for small problems.

Favored Enemy & Hunter's Mark Ranger 1

You always have Hunter's Mark prepared. It does not count against your prepared spells.

You can cast Hunter's Mark 2 times per Long Rest (= proficiency bonus) without expending a spell slot. Beyond those free casts, you may cast it using any 1st-level (or higher) spell slot you have.

Hunter's Mark: Bonus action. Mark a creature you can see within 90 ft. While the mark is up, your weapon attacks deal an extra 1d6 force damage to the marked target (once per attack roll). Up to 1 hour, concentration. When the marked target drops to 0 HP, you can use a Bonus Action on a later turn to move the mark to a new target.

Weapon Mastery Ranger 1

You can use the mastery property of 2 weapons at a time. You can swap your masteries on a Long Rest with one hour of practice per weapon.

Current masteries:

  • Shortsword (Vex): on hit, advantage on next attack roll vs same target before end of your turn.
  • Scimitar (Nick): when you make an extra attack from the Light property, you can do it as part of the Attack action — freeing your bonus action.
Spellcasting Ranger 1

Wisdom is your spellcasting ability. Spell Save DC 13, Spell Attack +5. Druidic Focus or holy symbol required.

You have 2 spell slots of 1st level, regained on a Long Rest.

Prepare 2 spells from the ranger spell list. Hunter's Mark is always prepared in addition to these. Swap one prepared spell on each Long Rest.

Currently prepared: Goodberry · Cure Wounds. Swap CW for Ensnaring Strike or Zephyr Strike if the party has a dedicated healer.

— Awaiting Level 3 — Beast Master Subclass · arrives at L3

The land formally blesses the war. When Dražen reaches Ranger 3, he chooses Beast Master. The wolf he raised — the same animal — finds him again.

Primal Companion (Beast of the Land). Medium beast (gnome can ride). 20 HP at L3, AC 16 (13 + WIS 3), +5 to hit, 1d8 + 5 force damage. Charge feature: 20+ ft straight movement → bite knocks Large-or-smaller prone (STR save DC 13).

Exceptional Training (L3). Bonus action commands the beast to take Strike, Help, Hide, or Dash. The beast also gets to make a Strike for free as part of the Attack action you take (in 2024 wording, verify with DM).

Free always-prepared spells: Longstrider, Speak with Animals.

Discuss with DM before session 1: (1) Mount + attack interaction (does the wolf retain its Strike while ridden?). (2) Continuity — the L3 Primal Companion is not a new wolf, it is the same one Dražen raised before the famine.

The Man

Dražen Korenić — late forties, 3'4", forty pounds, watermelon-shaped skull, hair the colour of dead grass, eyes the colour of a winter pond. Skin weathered like old harness leather. Hands scarred from forty years of working soil that no longer exists.

He worked the land his father worked. He married a woman named Mira. They had a daughter named Vesna, after the spring.

The elven opium operation came in stages. First the buyers offering coin for fields. Then the pressure. Then the seizures. Then the burnings. The poppies replaced the corn, one valley at a time. The famine started when there was nothing left to eat and nothing left to trade.

He lost Mira and Vesna to it — slowly, the way famine kills, not in one violent moment but in a long series of smaller surrenders. He tried opium himself first as numbness for grief, then as the only thing that would let him sleep, then as the thing that ate his mornings.

He retreated into the hills with a half-wild dog he'd raised from a pup, named for the shape on its forehead — Mrki ("the dark one"). The land kept him alive. He learned the old druidic tricks his grandmother knew: how to coax berries from a dead branch, how to read weather in birds, how to make the thorns answer when called. Something in the soil started talking to him. Or he went mad. Hard to say which.

He came down from the hills because the famine has now killed enough of his people that there's no one left to bury the dead. He's not here to save anyone. He's here to make the men who did this die badly.

Anchors

Bond. Mrki, the wolf-dog. Found me when I had no reason left to live. He is the only thing left that knew me before.

Ideal. The land is owed. The poppy must burn.

Flaw. Resin in the morning, or the world won't hold its shape.

Won't cross. Won't hurt children. Won't betray a comrade who's been honest with me.

Will cross. Will torture an elven slaver for information. Will poison a cartel well. Will leave a wounded enemy to die in the cold.

How it acts

In combat the beast moves on your initiative. On each of your turns you can use a Bonus Action to command it to take the Attack, Disengage, Dash, Dodge, Help, or Hide action. Without a command it can only Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or take a free Strike if it triggered Charge.
If reduced to 0 HP, it doesn't die — it simply leaves play. Bring it back with 1 hour of work after a Long Rest, or by spending a 1st-level slot during a Short or Long Rest.

Roadmap — spell prep at L2 / L3

Level 2. Slots: 2 · Prepared: 3. Add Two-Weapon Fighting style (+DEX to off-hand) and Deft Explorer (Expertise: Survival). Suggested third prep: Ensnaring Strike (lock a fleeing target).

Level 3. Slots: 3 · Prepared: 4 + 2 free subclass spells. Beast Master kicks in. Longstrider and Speak with Animals become always-prepared (do not count against limit). Suggested combat loadout:

  • Hunter's Mark (always-prep) — single-target damage tracker
  • Faerie Fire (MI free + slot) — swarm opener, party advantage
  • Goodberry — famine relief / triage
  • Cure Wounds — emergency hp burst (or swap for Zephyr Strike if healer)
  • Ensnaring Strike — lockdown alternative to HM
  • Zephyr Strike (if 2014 spells allowed) — burst + mobility execute

Tactical rule of thumb at L3 — Single tough target → HM. Group of meaningful enemies → Faerie Fire. Lock down a runner → Ensnaring Strike. Catch the lieutenant → Zephyr Strike.
